6 core flows

A voice AI layer comes after the WhatsApp pipeline — designed for the moments where typing falls short and tone, hesitation, and authenticity actually matter. This brief sequences the use cases in pipeline order, from first qualification to post-interview debrief.

The pipeline
1 Qualify → 2 Re-engage → 3 Recruiter → 4 Match Confirm → 5 Interview Prep → 6 Debrief

Core sequence

six flows, in pipeline order
01
Qualification & Verification
First voice touchpoint after WhatsApp onboarding
  • Availability confirmation — verbal "yes I can start Nov 15" catches hedging that text hides
  • Work auth deep-dive — visa type, expiry, sponsorship needs, relocation appetite
  • Rate negotiation — voice reads hesitation and probes "is X negotiable?"
  • Language fluency check — typed English ≠ spoken English; critical for client-facing roles
  • CV gap probing — "walk me through the 18-month gap in 2023"
02
Re-engagement
Dormant candidate availability refresh
  • Cold pool revival — voice gets answered when WhatsApp gets ignored
  • Maybe-available check-ins — every 2-4 weeks, light status pulse
  • Stuck-conversation rescue — escalation when the WhatsApp router detects loop or frustration
03
Recruiter-Side Calls
Voice as input from the demand side
  • JD intake call — capture the real requirements vs the sanitized JD
  • Match feedback — "why did you reject candidate X?" → calibrates the matching model
  • Daily standup — status of active roles in 90 seconds, by voice
  • Escalation calls — high-priority roles with no movement
04
Match Confirmation
After the client shows interest in the candidate
  • Genuine interest probe — distinguish "polite yes" from real enthusiasm
  • Conflict-of-interest disclosure — already interviewing elsewhere? notice period running?
  • Last-mile detail capture — preferred name, pronunciation, comms style
  • Recruiter pre-brief — what the client actually needs that wasn't in the JD
05
Interview Preparation
After the client confirms they will interview
  • Role & company briefing — team, product, interview format
  • Mock interview — practice likely questions, capture readiness signal
  • Weakness coaching — frame the gap, soften the rough edge
  • Day-of confirmation call — proactively reduce no-shows
06
Post-Interview Debrief
Last touchpoint in the core sequence
  • Candidate debrief — captures red flags before the recruiter pushes the offer
  • Recruiter debrief — structured feedback by voice, faster than a form
  • Refined salary expectations — post-interview anchor often shifts
  • Counter-offer scenario probing — "if your employer counters, what would it take to keep you?"
  • Post-placement check-ins — week 1 / month 1 / month 3 retention signal
◆ Separate flows

Out-of-sequence use cases

Different consent model, different scope.

  • Reference calls — calling the references the candidate provided; entirely different consent and disclosure flow
  • Identity / fraud check — light KYC voice match for high-trust placements; optional, unlikely near-term
✦ Optional

Cross-cutting capabilities

Capabilities, not standalone flows.

  • Post-offer deployment logistics — trip arrangements, visa processing, mobilization paperwork; rare, high-touch placements only
  • Multi-language — Bahasa, Tagalog, Mandarin for the SEA pool
  • Accessibility — for low-chat-engagement candidates
  • Sentiment & authenticity detection — coaching vs genuine, across all flows
  • Bulk activation campaigns — cold-call dormant pools at scale
  • GDPR-compliant recording — disclosed, consented, transcribed into _conversation
  • Outcome loop seeding — every voice call → structured signal feeding match calibration
